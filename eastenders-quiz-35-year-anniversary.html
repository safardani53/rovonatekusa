ANSWERS

1) Den Watts.

2) Kat and Alfie Moon (The six-part BBC drama saw the couple travel to Ireland in search of Kat’s long-lost son. It was not given a second series).

3) Ethel Skinner (Ethel became ill in September 2000, and asked her friend Dot to help her when the time finally came for her to pass away. She said: “You’re the best friend I ever had” to Dot before she died of an overdose.

4) Six different actors have played the role since his on-screen birth on March 21, 1996.

5) Peggy Mitchell.

6) Five times (making him the most married character in EastEnders’ history).

7) She said “How’s Adam?” instead of “How’s Ian?” (The actor who plays Ian Beale is called Adam Woodyatt).

8) 2013.

9) Princess (Den famously said “Hello, Princess” as his first line when he made a shock return from the dead in 2003).

10) Carol.

11) Nick Cotton.

12) Walford East.

13) Tracey (who is the longest-surviving “extra” in the soap. She works behind the bar at The Queen Vic).

14) Cindy Beale.

15) E20.

16) Kat Slater and Alfie Moon.

17) 22million.

18) Mark Fowler.

19) Melanie Healey.

20) She was hit by Frank Butcher’s car.

21) Robbie Jackson.

22) 2001.

23) Grant Mitchell.

24) Sean Slater (This storyline came in for heavy criticism as many people felt it was inappropriate to be screened before the watershed time of 9pm due to its content).

25) Lisa Shaw.

26) Emma Bunton.

27) Lacey Turner.

28) Anyone Can Fall In Love.

29) She was the first soap character to be featured in a whole episode entirely alone, known as a “single-hander”.

30) They fell from the roof of The Vic.

31) Mick Carter (who found out that Shirley Carter was actually his mum and not his sister in 2014).

32) Ronnie Mitchell (The storyline sparked more than 1,000 complaints to Ofcom).

33) She was struck by a lorry (Her character had been in the soap from 1998–2002 and 2018–2019 and was played by Tamzin Outhwaite).

34) Pat Butcher (She reappeared to comfort Peggy in her final moments).

35) Wellard (He first appeared on October 18, 1994, and became EastEnders’ longest-serving pet, before being killed off in August 2008).

36) It was the first British serial drama to feature a two-minute silence for Armistice Day.

37) Three (Ian Beale has been in the frame for the Who Shot Phil? Who Killed Archie? And Who Killed Lucy Beale? plots).

38) Nick Grimshaw (He appeared in several soaps in 2013 as part of a soap-bombing challenge set on his Breakfast Show. He made his brief appearance during a Christmas OAP party and was also spotted walking through the community centre).

39) Simon May.

40) McKlunky’s.

41) Phil.

42) The Walford Gazette.

43) Billy Mitchell.

44) Stephen Beale.

45) Liver (Phil Mitchell had the transplant over Christmas 2016 after years of alcoholism left him with severe organ damage).

46) Pauline Fowler.

47) Lord  Sugar.

48) Tina Carter.

49) The London Eye.

50) Gretna Green.
