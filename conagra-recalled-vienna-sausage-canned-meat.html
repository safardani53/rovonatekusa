CONAGRA has recalled more than 2.5million pounds of canned meat after contamination was discovered, according to the U.S. Department of Agriculture’s Food Safety and Inspection Safety (USDA).

The meat’s brand decided to recall its canned Vienna sausage and chicken products because some of the cans were contaminated during the packaging process.

The recall also affects Vienna sausages sold under brands like Goya.

Shoppers regularly find the Conagra canned sausage and chicken in stores like Walmart and Kroger.

An individual can sells for between $2 and $3, while a package of 18 cans is around $40.

"Retailers have been informed of the recall and are removing the impacted items from shelves," a spokesperson from Conagra told The U.S. Sun.

If you purchased the product under the brand names Armour, Kroger, Goya, Prairie Belt, Hargis House, Grace, or Great Value, it's possible your meat was affected.

“Subsequent investigation by the establishment determined that the cans subject to recall may have been damaged in a manner that is not readily apparent to consumers, which may allow foodborne pathogens to enter the cans,” the USDA said in a statement.

Shoppers are encouraged to throw out their canned meat if it was processed between December 12 and January 13.

You can tell if this happened based on the establishment number of P4247 located on the can.

A popular sunscreen product has also been recalled after cancer-causing chemicals were discovered.

After Banana Boat reviewed its products, it was revealed that some samples contained trace levels of the chemical benzene.

Benzene is classified as a human carcinogen, according to the Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

Too much exposure to this particular chemical may cause life-threatening cancers including leukemia, a blood cancer of the bone marrow and blood disorders.

Those with the recalled product should immediately stop using it.

Consumers should contact their physician or healthcare provider if they have any questions, concerns or are experiencing any problems related to using the sunscreen.

Every model of the Rock ‘n Play Sleepers has been recalled since the deaths.

They were sold from September 2009 through April 2019 for between $40 and $149.

Since then, 70 more infant deaths have been reported including at least eight fatalities after the initial recall announcement.
