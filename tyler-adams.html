Career

  • Adams was signed by the New York Red Bulls II, the club’s senior reserve side in the United Soccer League, on March 19, 2015. On April 4, 2015, Adams made his debut appearance for the team in a 4-1 victory against Toronto FC II, the club’s first win.
  • Adams was a regular starter for NYRB II throughout the 2016 season, and he helped the club win the USL Cup Final 5-1 over Swope Park Rangers.
  • On July 23, 2015, Adams made his New York Red Bulls debut against Premier League champions Chelsea in a friendly match. He headed the ball past Asmir Begovic in the 69th minute to give the Red Bulls a 4-2 win.
  • Adams made his senior team debut in preseason training camp the following year after signing a contract on November 3, 2015.
    On April 1, 2016, Adams was an unused substitute in the New England Revolution’s 1-0 victory against the Los Angeles Galaxy.
  • He made his MLS debut on April 13, 2016, starting against the San Jose Earthquakes. He was subsequently loaned back to Red Bulls II for the remainder of the season.
  • In 2017, he established himself as a key member of the Red Bulls’ first-team roster. Adams provided Bradley Wright-Phillips’ 77th-minute equalizer as New York overcame FC Cincinnati 3-2 on August 15, 2017.
  • With the win, New York advanced to the Open Cup final for the first time since 2003. On September 27, 2017, Adams scored his first two MLS goals in a 3-3 draw with D.C. United.
  • Adams scored the opening goal in New York’s 3-1 CONCACAF Champions League quarterfinal win against Club Tijuana on March 13, 2018.
  • Adams has represented the United States at the under-15, under-17, and under-20 levels. By playing in all of his team’s games in the Concacaf Under-17 Championships in Honduras, he helped the United States qualify for the 2015 FIFA Under-17 World Cup.
  • Adams started every game for the United States at the 2017 FIFA Under-20 World Cup in South Korea. Adams earned his first senior national team cap on November 14th, 2017, in a 1-1 game against Portugal.
  • Tyler Adams scored a goal in a friendly against Mexico on September 11, 2018, at Nissan Stadium in Nashville, Tennessee.
  • Adams was named to the 2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup roster in June but withdrew due to an injury.
