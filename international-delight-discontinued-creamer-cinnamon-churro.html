International Delight confirms popular creamer is discontinued and customers say they have 'switched

March 18, 2024
blog

INTERNATIONAL Delight has confirmed that a popular creamer flavor is now history.

The company has one of the most popular coffee creamer brands seen in households today.

International Delight currently offers a variety of different flavors from traditional ones like French vanilla to seasonal options such as pumpkin pie spice.

But one customer recently inquired about the cinnamon churro creamer.

A user on X tweeted at International Delight: "Please tell me the cinnamon churro creamer isn’t discontinued?"

"Haven’t seen any in stores the past few weeks and it’s not on your site."

READ MORE ON DISCONTINUED

And International Delight confirmed cinnamon churro creamer has been axed as it was only a limited edition flavor.

The fan said that the cannoli flavor can no longer be found in stores as well – but it's unclear if that variety has been axed as well.

In retaliation, the fan has decided to switch to rival Coffee-Mate.

While prices can vary by location, the cinnamon churro creamer was last seen selling in a 32-ounce container at Walmart for $3.77.

Some of the ingredients consist of sugar, palm oil, water, carrageenan, as well as natural and artificial flavors.

Also, there were five grams of added sugar per serving.

Overall, Walmart customers rated it 3.3 stars out of five.

OTHER ALTERNATIVES TO BUY

Customers who were a fan of the cinnamon flavoring may have another option.

However, they may have to go off-brand.

For example, Walmart's Great Value brand makes a honey cinnamon and vanilla flavor.

It sells in a 32-fluid-ounce bottle for only $2.68.

Also, dairy-free brand nutpods makes an unsweetened cinnamon swirl flavor.

It is available at Walmart in a 16-ounce container for $3.97.

OTHER DISCONTINUED CREAMERS

Also, International Delight and other coffee creamer brands may have some more bad news for customers.

International Delight has gotten rid of its one touch latte variety of products.

They aimed to turn a coffee into a latte in a matter of seconds.

According to the instructions, customers only needed to fill up their cups with two-thirds of coffee before shaking and applying the topping.

The cans come in vanilla, mocha, and caramel flavors.

International Delight has also gotten rid of the chocolate macadamia flavor.

The Cold Stone Creamery flavor has been discontinued, as well.

When it comes to rival Coffee-Mate, the company confirmed that its cookies 'n cocoa flavor has been axed.

The flavor consisted of seed oils, natural and artificial flavors, and carrageenan.

Also, Silk has discontinued its entire unsweetened creamer lineup.

It included a variety of flavors such as almond.
