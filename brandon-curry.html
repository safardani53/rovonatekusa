Early Life & Family 

The professional builder, Brandon grew up in Nashville, Tennessee, USA, and has Afro-American ethnicity.

Sadly, there is not much information about his father, mother, and siblings.

Talking about his career path, Curry got into weight training after receiving Hulk Hogan dumbbells on his sixth birthday.

According to him, his parents first put him in gymnastics classes as a kid. But, he was not interested in gymnastics at all.

Education

Diving a bit into his education, Mr. Olympia attended Hunters Lane Comprehensive High School. He also played wrestling, track and field, and football while studying.

Most importantly, Brandon earned his bachelor’s degree in exercise science from Middle Tennessee State University. 

To add on, Mr. Olympia started playing football in college. But later, he switched his interest to bodybuilding.

Moreover, after graduation, he started working on weight exercises. Eventually, Curry became a personal trainer and later got into professional bodybuilding.

Bodybuilding Career

Moving to his career section, he officially debuted as a bodybuilder in 2003. He not only competed but also won the first event he took part in.

Following that, Curry gained more confidence and started improving his body physique. As a result, he participated in more amateur shows every year.

Furthermore, Brandon competed in the NPC Junior National Championships in the light-heavyweight division in June 2006. He weighed 189 pounds during the event.

In fact, he put an extra 28 pounds of weight to boot and upgraded for the 2007 NPC USA Championships. There, he grabbed the second position in the heavyweight division.

However, Brandon won both the heavyweight division and the overall NPC USA Championships in 2008.

Additionally, he stood in the 8th position at the 2017 IFBB Mr. Olympia. That same year, he won the IFBB Arnold Pro Classic held in Australia.

The New Mr. Olympia

As we all know, Mr. Olympia was what pushed Curry to the limelight; also making him a household name. 

Not to mention, participating in the Mr. Olympia competitions is itself a win.

Likewise, that same Mr. Olympia 2019 was held on September 14, in Las Vegas.

Without a doubt, the 41 years old Curry impressed the judges with his excellent physique, muscular body, and arm size.

After a decade in the bodybuilding career, finally, Brandon held the trophy of 15th Mr. Olympia. It was surely a tear-jerking moment for him and his supporters. 

Prior to this, Brandon won the Arnold Classic trophy. It is also considered the second most prestigious bodybuilding event.

So, everyone is expecting him to be the front-runner for the next Olympia.

But the competition is surely not that easy!

For that, Curry has to compete with last year’s third and fourth-place winner, Hadi Choopa. He is widely known as Persian Wolf.

Bodybuilding Diet and Workout Routine

The elite athlete, Brandon was into bodybuilding from a young age. Moreover, young Curry was always active in sports such as wrestling and rugby since his school days.

Later, Brandon joined a local gym and started gaining muscles. To boot, he was also focused on diet routine and nutrition.

As per his plan, Brandon follows low carbs and high-protein diet.

To sum up, Brandon’s diet consists of supplements like protein, multivitamins, and fat burners, which help increase immune power.

Moreover, nowadays, Curry maintains his lean physique by working out five days a week in the gym. But, the workout routine changes periodically according to the competition season.

Over and above that, his main focus is on weight training. He loves hamstring training, pull-ups, and t-bar row deadlifts.

Brandon Curry Silences Critics

The American-born, Brandon had to hear negative comments like “he is not the faithful Mr. Olympia” after his victory in September 2019.

Many fans expressed their opinion like how he has to defeat Shawn Rhoden or Phil Heath to say he is a valid Mr. Olympia.

In response to those comments, Brandon wrote,

“I’m the guy with nothing to lose since all of you undermine my win anyway, so I’m thankful for the opportunity to see how I can do against him.” 

Instead of losing his temper, Brandon responded with his perfect answer to the critics. As expected of Mr. Olympia!

Whatever the critics say, he is not letting those people divert his focus from training.
