Trent Green | Career

Early Career

As for his early career, Trent used to play football for the Indiana University Hoosiers. He played in the 1991 Copper Bowl and dominated Baylor, a highly regarded team, 24-0.

As Green led the team, the game’s performance was regarded as the most impressive performance of that season.

During his four-year career at the university, Trent threw for 5,400 yards with 31 interceptions and 23 touchdowns.

Green graduated from the university with a degree in business and started looking forward to his NFL career.

Professional Career

In 1993, Green was drafted by the San Diego Chargers as the 26th pick in the eighth round. However, he did not get to play on the field during that season.

The British Columbia Lions of the Canadian Football League in 1994 cut him. So, he joined the Washington Redskins.

Hence, he was disconnected from the NFL for quite some time, he threw for 3,441 yards with 11 interceptions and 23 touchdowns in Redskins.

Furthermore, just in time, he became an unrestricted free agent in the 1998 season. That year he did not accept a four-year deal from Redskins, valued at $4 million then. 

Career In Rams

After his time with Redskins, he agreed to a 4-year $17.5 million contract with the St. Louis Rams, which included a $4.5 million signing bonus.

However, he could not play for the Rams that season because of his injury during a preseason game.

Trent started the 2000 season as a backup to Warner, the reigning NFL MVP. The two combined led the Rams to the highest team passing yards total in NFL history.

But, Green was traded during the offseason to the Kansas City Chiefs for the 12th overall pick in the 2001 NFL Draft.

A career In Kansas City

In his first season in Kansas City, Green threw for 3,783 yards and 17 touchdowns, and 24 interceptions. This was a bad performance from him. However, Green improved his games in 2002, throwing 26 touchdowns to only 13 interceptions.

Trent also tied the record for the longest career pass play in the NFL history by passing for 99 yards in the year 2002.

In 2003, he threw for 4,039 yards with 24 touchdowns and 12 interceptions. Similarly, in 2004, Green passed for 4,591 yards, 17 interceptions, and 27 touchdowns, phenomenal play.

Trent had a strong season in 2005 where he threw for 4,010 yards and threw just ten interceptions.

This time he got elected to his second Pro Bowl. Green had four consecutive seasons with a QB rating of 90 or better, which is quite impressive. That must be one of the reasons he was selected for the Pro Bowl.

In a dreadful game in 2006 for Green against Cincinnati Bengal, Trent suffered from a severe concussion. He was unconscious for 15 minutes during the game and was stated as not fit to play football for the time being.

At the end of 2006, he only played where Kansas lost against Peyton Manning and the Indianapolis Colts in the playoffs. Hence, his career in Kansas City ended that year.

A career In Miami Dolphins

In 2007, the Chiefs traded Green to the Miami Dolphins. However, his start with Miami Dolphins was jeopardized in October when he suffered another severe concussion in a game against the Texas Houston.

This event made his fans call for his retirement, but Trent was back with the team in practice.

But he was ruled unfit to play and was announced to be placed on injured reserve on October 20, 2007. Soon he was released by the Dolphins on February 11, 2008.

Back With Rams

After his release from the Dolphins, Green agreed to a 3-year deal worth about $9 million with the St. Louis Rams.

In September 2008, Trent was named the starter against Buffalo Bills. Trent completed 17 32 passes for 236 yards with one interception and a quarterback rating of 64.1.
