She cost more than a quarter of a million quid and repaid a sliver of that when third on debut at Newmarket.

The booking of Ryan Moore takes the eye and her middle-distance pedigree suggests this trip will suit as a juvenile.

Gaasyah comes from Richard Hannon who does well with youngsters here.

She is related to two-year-old winners over this trip and a market move would be telling.

Oisin Murphy and Andrew Balding team up with Imperial Express who is from a sprinting family and should know her job.

Desperate Hero is consistent and went down in a photo finish at Yarmouth last time.

He’s back from the same mark and is another who has run well here before.

Holkham Bay is having just his thrid run and went close on his turf debut at Doncaster last time.

He should build on that and looks on a fair weight.

JM Jungle is having a good season and followed a Hamilton second with a game win at Haydock.

This trip is ideal and he could improve past a 5lb rise in the weights.

Estate got the job done well at Windsor last time and was third on his last visit here.

Thunder Moor has been tried at Listed level and went close in a Yarmouth handicap seven days ago.
