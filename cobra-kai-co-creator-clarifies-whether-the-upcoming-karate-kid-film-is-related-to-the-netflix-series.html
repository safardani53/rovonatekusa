Cobra Kai Co-Creator Clarifies Whether The Upcoming Karate Kid Film Is Related To The Netflix Series

April 05, 2024
blog

To put an end to suspicions over whether Netflix’s Cobra Kai is linked to the next Karate Kid film, which is planned to be released in 2024, the series’ co-creator Jon Hurwitz said that he doesn’t “know anything about it” and that it is unrelated to the fan-favorite Netflix program. The fifth season of Cobra Kai aired on Netflix on September 9, 2022, to overwhelmingly good reviews from both fans and reviewers, with praise focusing on the plot’s entertainment value, great writing, and performances by the cast. Continue reading to hear more about Jon Hurwitz’s opinions on the upcoming Karate Kid film and other topics.

The co-creator of Cobra Kai explains that the series has nothing to do with the new Karate Kid.

Jon Hurwitz said in the tweet quoted above that he’d “love to do Karate Kid and Cobra Kai movies and hope to eventually.” He added that the previously announced Karate Kid film is “not from us or centered on the Cobra Kai actors.” Sony Columbia Pictures has officially confirmed a new Karate Kid picture for June 7, 2024, just days after the fifth season of Cobra Kai was published on Netflix.

At this time, no information regarding the narrative, actors, or crew is available. Because of the date of the announcement, many fans on social media speculated on whether the film is related to the renowned Netflix program. Fans may be a little upset now that Jon Hurwitz has confirmed that the two projects are unrelated. Several major events occurred during the fifth season of the renowned Netflix series. More About the Cobra Kai cast

Daniel La Russo is played by Ralph Macchio in the series. Macchio previously portrayed La Russo in the Karate Kid films, for which he gained widespread acclaim from both fans and reviewers. Aside from the Netflix action series and Karate Kid films, Macchio is well-known for his roles in programs such as The Outsiders, Ugly Betty, and Deuce, to mention a few. Several other notable performers, including William Zabka, Thomas Ian Griffith, Martin Kove, Yuji Okumoto, and Sean Kanan, play crucial supporting parts in the series. Netflix has the program accessible for viewing.
